# Legō — Curriculum: Scope & Sequence

A 30-unit reading course: zero Latin → reading lightly-adapted real authors. Three stages of ten
units. Grammar is introduced for **recognition** (decode it when you see it), never for production.
Each unit = grammar sections + ~25 vocabulary items + 2 story readings + comprehension questions + quiz.

## The story

A continuing narrative carries the whole course.

**Setting:** Ostia, the port of Rome, around AD 110; later Rome itself.

**Cast (use these names and spellings consistently):**
- **Mārcus Fabius** ("Mārcus") — the father, a grain merchant (mercātor)
- **Līvia** — the mother, sharp and practical
- **Quīntus** — their son, 12, curious, wants to be a sailor
- **Paulla** — their daughter, 8, fearless
- **Ferōx** — the family dog, a lovable menace who steals food
- **Titus** — Līvia's brother, a ship's captain (magister nāvis), full of sea stories
- **Philō** — a Greek tutor (paedagōgus) who joins in Stage 3 to teach Quīntus to read the authors

Stage 1 is domestic life in Ostia. Stage 2 is a family journey to Rome and back. Stage 3 is a
storm-and-recovery arc that hands off into reading real (adapted) texts with Philō as the frame.

## Stage I — Fundāmenta (units 1–10)

1. **Salvē!** — *Grammar:* Latin has no "a/the"; pronunciation & alphabet essentials; sentences with «est/sunt»; nominative subjects; negation «nōn»; «et». *Vocab theme:* greetings, family members, house basics. *Story:* meet the familia Fabia and their house in Ostia.
2. **Domus et Hortus** — *Grammar:* 1st & 2nd declension nominatives, singular & plural; predicate adjectives with est/sunt (recognition); «‑que». *Theme:* house, garden, everyday objects. *Story:* a tour of the house and garden; Ferōx digs up Līvia's flowers.
3. **Vīta Cotīdiāna** — *Grammar:* present tense, 3rd person sg./pl. (‑t/‑nt) of 1st/2nd conjugation; basic word order (verb often last). *Theme:* daily actions, work, rest. *Story:* morning in the household — everyone at their tasks; Ferōx sleeps.
4. **Ad Macellum** — *Grammar:* accusative case (1st/2nd decl.), sg. & pl.; direct objects. *Theme:* market, food. *Story:* Līvia and Paulla shop at the market; Ferōx steals a sausage.
5. **Portus** — *Grammar:* genitive case (possession, "of"). *Theme:* harbor, ships, belongings, family relations. *Story:* Uncle Titus's ship arrives; whose cargo is whose.
6. **Dōna** — *Grammar:* dative case (indirect object) with verbs of giving/showing/telling. *Theme:* gifts, telling & showing. *Story:* Titus hands out gifts and sea stories.
7. **Per Viās Ostiae** — *Grammar:* prepositions + ablative (in, cum, sine, ā/ab, ē/ex, dē) and + accusative (ad, per, prope, in = "into"). *Theme:* town, places, movement. *Story:* a walk through Ostia to the forum and harbor.
8. **In Lūdō** — *Grammar:* present tense, all six persons (1st/2nd conj.); personal pronouns ego/tū/nōs/vōs; «‑ne» questions; answering with «ita/minimē». *Theme:* school, speaking, numbers of people. *Story:* Quīntus at school; a dialogue with the magister.
9. **Epistula ex Urbe** — *Grammar:* 3rd declension nouns, all cases (recognition of the endings). *Theme:* the city, people, body, letters. *Story:* a letter arrives from Rome; Titus describes the great city.
10. **Ferōx Perditus** — *Grammar:* 1st/2nd declension adjectives, agreement across cases; substantives; Stage 1 review. *Theme:* qualities, colors, sizes. *Story:* Ferōx goes missing and is found at the harbor; the family resolves to visit Rome. Quiz doubles as a stage review.

## Stage II — Via (units 11–20)

11. **Cōnsilium** — *Grammar:* sum/esse full present; possum + complementary infinitive; infinitive forms of known conjugations. *Theme:* ability, wishing, planning, travel gear. *Story:* preparing the journey to Rome.
12. **Nāvigāmus** — *Grammar:* 3rd, 3rd‑iō, and 4th conjugation present (all persons). *Theme:* sailing, river, loading. *Story:* up the Tiber on Titus's boat.
13. **Ōlim** — *Grammar:* imperfect tense (all conjugations + eram/poteram); time adverbs tum/ōlim/saepe. *Theme:* memories, childhood. *Story:* on the boat, Mārcus remembers his boyhood.
14. **Rōma!** — *Grammar:* perfect tense (all conjugations + fuī/potuī). *Theme:* arrival, sights, wonder. *Story:* they arrive in Rome; a day of firsts, narrated in the perfect.
15. **Haec Urbs** — *Grammar:* demonstratives is/ea/id, hic, ille; possessives meus/tuus/noster/vester/suus. *Theme:* monuments, city sights. *Story:* sightseeing — this temple, that arch, the Circus Maximus.
16. **Quō Īs?** — *Grammar:* question words quis/quid/cūr/ubi/quō/quandō/quōmodo; imperatives (recognition); vocative. *Theme:* directions, asking and answering. *Story:* Quīntus gets lost in Rome and must ask his way home.
17. **Fortior Quam Leō** — *Grammar:* 3rd declension adjectives; comparatives & superlatives; quam. *Theme:* spectacle, courage, comparison. *Story:* a day at the races and the amphitheater.
18. **Quot?** — *Grammar:* numbers 1–100 & Roman numerals; time expressions (ablative "when", accusative "how long"). *Theme:* time, money, buying & selling. *Story:* Mārcus does business in Rome; Paulla counts everything.
19. **Vir Quī…** — *Grammar:* relative clauses (quī, quae, quod). *Theme:* describing people & things. *Story:* a mysterious merchant who claims to know Titus.
20. **Domum** — *Grammar:* future tense (all conjugations + erō/poterō); Stage 2 review. *Theme:* plans, promises. *Story:* the journey home; Titus will sail for Greece; Quīntus dreams of the sea. Quiz doubles as a stage review.

## Stage III — Ad Auctōrēs (units 21–30)

21. **Portus Labōrat** — *Grammar:* passive voice, present system (present/imperfect/future); ablative of agent ā/ab. *Theme:* work, cargo, the harbor machine. *Story:* back in Ostia — goods are carried, ships are loaded, everything is done by somebody.
22. **Tempestās** — *Grammar:* perfect passive (PPP + sum); 4th & 5th declension nouns (manus, portus, rēs, diēs). *Theme:* weather, sea, loss. *Story:* a storm; a ship is wrecked; cargo is lost — and Titus was aboard.
23. **Nāve Āmissā** — *Grammar:* participles (present active, perfect passive, future active); ablative absolute. *Theme:* crisis, response, hope. *Story:* the family, the ship having been lost, waits for news.
24. **Nūntius** — *Grammar:* pluperfect & future perfect; deponent verbs (recognition: loquitur, sequitur, cōnātur…). *Theme:* news, speech, searching. *Story:* word arrives — Titus had reached Sardinia before the storm broke.
25. **Titum Vīvere!** — *Grammar:* indirect statement (accusative + infinitive, all infinitive tenses). *Theme:* messages, reports, believing. *Story:* letters and rumors fly; a messenger says that Titus lives; reunion.
26. **Ut Discat** — *Grammar:* subjunctive, present & imperfect; purpose (ut/nē) and result clauses (recognition). *Theme:* intention, learning, teaching. *Story:* Philō the tutor arrives so that Quīntus may learn to read the authors.
27. **Fābulae** — *Grammar:* subjunctive, perfect & pluperfect; cum-clauses; indirect questions. *Reading:* adapted Phaedrus fables (e.g. the Wolf and the Lamb; the Fox and the Grapes) framed by Philō's lessons.
28. **Rōmulus et Remus** — *Grammar:* conditions; gerund/gerundive (recognition only); irregular verbs ferō, eō, volō/nōlō/mālō, fīō. *Reading:* adapted Roman legends (Romulus & Remus, and one more) in the frame story.
29. **Caesar** — *Grammar:* reading strategy for real authors — long sentences, delayed verbs, common idioms; review of the whole verb system. *Reading:* lightly adapted Caesar (on Britain or Gaul) and Eutropius.
30. **Lege!** — *Grammar:* course review; how to keep going (what to read next, and how). *Reading:* a lightly adapted Vulgate passage (e.g. the Prodigal Son) and two short Martial epigrams. *Story:* epilogue — Quīntus, grown, reads on the deck of his own ship. Final quiz doubles as a course exam.

## Standing notes for authors

- Grammar available to unit N = everything listed for units 1…N above, nothing beyond it.
- «est/sunt», «nōn», «et», predicate adjectives: available from unit 1–2 onward. «inquit» for
  dialogue: any unit, glossed.
- Readings recycle old grammar and vocab relentlessly; new grammar should appear ≥ 8 times across
  a unit's two readings.
- Stage 3 units 27–30: the "readings" are the adapted texts themselves; keep a light frame
  (Philō and Quīntus discussing them) in the intro lines or a short first paragraph.
- Keep the humor gentle and the stakes human: food stolen by Ferōx, a child lost and found, a
  storm survived. No graphic violence.
